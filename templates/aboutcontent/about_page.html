About Extrasynaptome

The project

The aim of the Collaborative Extrasynaptome Data Repository (CEDaR) is to centralise and organise all current data on chemical interactions between neurons that occur outside of synaptic connections in the nervous system, with participation from the wider neuroscience community, and provide visualisation and analysis tools to reveal the hidden “wireless” networks of long-range interactions between neurons that sit alongside the “wired” synaptic connectome.

Starting with the monoamine and neuropeptide networks of the roundworm Caenorhabditis elegans, this project is the first centralised resource for studying a class of critically important biological networks. With the potential for expansion to cover multiple species, this project aspires to serve an international user base to support and accelerate research in neuroscience.

The mission

The aim is to develop an online resource to curate and centralise data related to extrasynaptic signalling, starting with C. elegans, entailing:

  • a database and ontology to organise the available data,
  • a tool for network analysis and visualisation to assist in profiling the routes of extrasynaptic transmission, and
  • a community portal to engage wider participation in the curation effort.

The principles

Information

To collect and organise the latest research findings with input from the community, to ensure an accurate and up-to-date resource that can be transformed into new knowledge and scientific insights.

Community

To engage with the community of researchers by allowing them to contribute what is known and to shed a light on what is unknown. As a community-driven project, data submissions, corrections, and recommendations are an integral part of the project. We warmly invite all interested parties to contribute.

People

To support the neuroscience and wider bioscience communities by enhancing scientific curation, research, and discovery in the area of neural signalling and connectomics.

The founder

Dr Barry Bentley

‐ Founder of Extrasynaptome Data Repository project

‐ Lead of the EUREKA Bio-AI laboratory and Bioengineering Research Group at Cardiff Metropolitan University in Cardiff

About me:

Dr Barry Bentley is the founder of the Extrasynaptome Data Repository project and a Senior Lecturer in the Cardiff School of Technologies.

He completed his Ph.D. at the University of Cambridge, where he worked at the MRC Laboratory of Molecular Biology on mapping and analysing the aminergic and peptidergic networks of C. elegans, which formed the basis of this project. He has also conducted research and consultancy work for multiple organisations including the University of Oxford, the European Space Agency, and ARM Holdings. Dr Bentley currently leads the EUREKA Bio-AI lab and the Bioengineering Research Group at Cardiff Metropolitan University and sits on the scientific advisory and review boards of several organisations.
