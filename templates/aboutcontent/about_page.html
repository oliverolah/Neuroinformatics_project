About Extrasynaptome

The project

Extrasynaptome Data Repository is a web application concentrated on network neuroscience and connectomics of the nervous systems ‐ such as of a 1mm long roundworm called Caenorhabditis elegans. That is to aid bioengineering research focused on human beingâ€™s brain functioning. The web application will act as a centralized database repository that will be able to curate, store and visualise the wired and wireless connectomes of neuron network systems to perform network analyses and expand on the already existing knowledge.

This project is the first centralised resource for studying a class of critically important biological networks. With the potential for expansion and the aim to form a larger grant application, this project aspires to serve an international userbase to support and accelerate research in multiple subfields of neuroscience.

The mission

The aim is to develop an online resource to curate and centralise data related to extrasynaptic signalling in e.g. C. elegans, entailing:

  • a database and ontology to organise the available data
  • a tool for network analysis and visualisation to assist in profiling the routes of extrasynaptic transmission, and
  • a community portal to engage wider participation in the curation effort

By developing this repository, it is hoped that the healthcare system shall be benefited ‐ more specifically the areas of Neurology and psychiatry to minimise the costs and enhance ineffective treatments by gathering enough information to aid the competent people in their work to accelerate the quality of patient treatment.

The principles

Information

To pursue the quality of the information given by the community to the community transformed into new knowledge and insights enhancing the processes for its experts.

Community

To engage with the community of competent people by allowing them to contribute to what is known and to shed a light on what is unknown. The community represents a source of information from which new inspirations arise.

People

To help the patients who are in need by enhancing the healthcare treatments and other related processes thanks to the quality of the information and competent community of experts.

The founder

Dr Barry Bentley

‐ Founder of Extrasynaptome Data Repository project

‐ Senior Lecturer in Computer Science at Cardiff Metropolitan University in Cardiff

About me:

I completed and received my Ph.D. at the University of Cambridge where I worked at the MRC Laboratory of Molecular Biology in the areas of computational neuroscience and bioinformatics. I have conducted research and consultancy works for multiple organisations including the Open University, the University of Oxford, the European Space Agency, and ARM Holdings. But my current research interests lie in the development and application of computational tools for biological and medical problems, with a particular interest in the areas of neuroscience, ageing, and cryobiology.

This web application is a continuation of my interest which sparked when I started to work on my Ph.D. thesis. By accumulating the knowledge and all of my ideas I had that the time I decided to produce a technology that would allow me to pursue the path of research in an attempt to contribute to the field of medicine. Through extensive research, the results had shown me that the fields of neurology and psychiatry suffer from high costs to treat its patients (due to the ageing population) in the UK due to inefficient treatments preceded by the lack of quality technological tools and data information.

By developing Extrasynaptome Data Repository I hope that the field of medicine will benefit from it. My wish for this project is to transform it into a robust and flexible bank of data information from which competent people shall be able to make their qualified conclusions. Through this technology, I would want to improve the environment in which the patients shall be treated under better treatment procedures with experts backed by reliable, accessible and up‐to‐date information. Let this project be the beginning of that.
